The person behind the writing

Shubham Kumar

Nonprofit Leader · AI Explorer · Structured Thinker

I'm 31, and I work in a nonprofit organisation in a leadership role. My day job involves strategy, teams, donors, and the constant pressure to prove impact in systems not designed for it.

This site is where I think out loud. I write long-form essays on leadership, the honest failures and hard-won lessons from the social sector, the possibilities and limits of AI, and the craft of making better decisions over time.

I started Mariner's Memoir because I was frustrated by content that was either too academic to be useful or too surface-level to be interesting. I'm trying to write the thing I wish I could read.

Why I write

The point of this

Writing is how I figure out what I actually think. The essays on this site aren't finished positions — they're the best version of my reasoning at the time I wrote them. Some will age poorly. I'm okay with that.

I care about three things in writing: honesty, specificity, and usefulness. Generic insight is the enemy. If I can't tell you something concrete — a framing, a pattern, a real example — I don't publish it.

I also try to write for people who are actually doing things, not people who are merely thinking about doing things. There's a difference. The reader I imagine is someone who runs something — a team, a program, an organisation — and is trying to do it better.

"I write to discover what I think. I publish to find out if I'm the only one who thinks it."

The name — Mariner's Memoir — comes from an image I've always found resonant: someone navigating open water, keeping a log not because they have all the answers, but because the act of recording forces clarity. That's what this is.

If something here is useful to you, I'd love to know. If something is wrong, I'd love to know even more.
